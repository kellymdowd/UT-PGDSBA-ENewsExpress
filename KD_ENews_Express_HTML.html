Project Business Statistics: E-news Express¶

Marks: 60

Define Problem Statement and Objectives¶

E-news Express believes that their decline in monthly subscribers is because the webpage is not well-designed and doesn't recommend content to keep customer engaged long enough to subscribe. The E-news Express design team has created a new landing page for their website in the hopes that it will be more effective in engaging customers and getting them to subscribe. Our data science team has conducted an A/B test and we will be perfoming the statistical analysis to answer four key questions along the way:

  1. Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than on the existing landing page?
  2. Is the conversion rate (the proportion of users who visit the landing page and get converted) for the new page greater than the conversion rate for the old page?
  3. Does the converted status depend on the preferred language?
  4. Is the time spent on the new page the same for the different language users?

Step 1: Define the null and alternate hypotheses¶

We will test the null hypothesis

$H_0:\mu_1=\mu_2$

against the alternate hypothesis

$H_a:\mu_1>\mu_2$

Where $\mu_1$ is the mean time spent on the new landing page and $\mu_2$ is the mean time spent on the old landing page.

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

We will use a two sample T-test because it meets the following criteria:

  • Continuous data - The time spent on the page is measured on a continuous scale.
  • Normally distributed populations or Sample sizes > 30 - Since the sample sizes are greater than 30, Central Limit Theorem states that the distribution of sample means will be normal.
  • Independent populations - As we are taking samples for two different channels, the two samples are from two independent populations.
  • Unequal population standard deviations - Since the sample stds are different, we can assume the population stds are also different.
  • Random sampling from the population - the collected sample is a simple random sample.

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

We have been told to use a 5% significance level so will set alpha as 0.05

Step 7: Draw inference¶

We have enough statistical evidence to reject the null hypothesis and can conclude that the mean time visitors spent on the new landing page is higher than the time visitors spent on the old landing page.

4. Is the time spent on the new page same for the different language users?¶

Step 1: Define the null and alternate hypotheses¶

Let $\mu_1, \mu_2, \mu_3$ be the means of time spent on the new page for users with preferred languages of english, spanish, and french respectively.

We will test the null hypothesis

$H_0: \mu_1 = \mu_2 = \mu_3$

against the alternative hypothesis

$H_a: $ At least one mean time spent on the new page is different from the rest.

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

We will use a One-way ANOVA Test. One variable is discrete and the other is continuous.

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

We have been told to use a 5% significance level so will set alpha as 0.05

Conclusion and Business Recommendations¶

We found that users who spent more time on the page were more likely to subscribe. We also found that users who saw the new landing page spent more time on the page than users who were shown the old landing page. We then validated that by concluding that users who were shown the new landing page had a higher conversion rate than users shown the old landing page.

The next thing we wanted to check was to see if users with different preferred languages of English, Spanish, & French had an impact on the amount of time they spent on the site and if the preferred language was independent of conversion. We found that there was no statistical difference in the amount of time users with different preferred languages spent on the site, and conversion was not dependent on language preference.

Therefore, it is recommended to implement the new landing page as-is with the same, current functionality in place for users of different preferred languages. With the implementation, E-news Express can expect to see more site visitors subscribing.
